Velocity Basics

What Velocity is and how to navigate it as a VA.

What is Velocity
Primary Purpose
Velocity is a mortgage deal creation, CRM, and lender submission platform. It handles the full mortgage workflow — creating deals, managing borrower data, collecting documents, and submitting to lenders. It is not limited to just CRM or just submission — it does both.
Full-cycle platform: CRM + submission in one
Key Navigation Tabs
Deals tab: All active and archived deals — your primary working area. Tasks: Upcoming deadlines, follow-ups, and reminders. Dashboard: Overview of pipeline activity. Contacts: All borrower and referral partner records.
Deals tab = your daily working area
Tracking Deadlines and Follow-ups
The Tasks feature in Velocity is the correct tool for tracking upcoming deadlines, follow-ups, and reminders — not the Calendar, not Notifications, not Reports. Create tasks for every follow-up action and check your task list at the start of each shift.

Deal Creation & Borrower Info

How to start a new deal and where each type of borrower information goes.

Creating a New Deal
What to Enter First
When creating a new deal in Velocity, the first fields to complete are deal type and client name. You cannot proceed without these two. Deal type determines which fields are required throughout the file. Client name links the deal to the borrower's contact record.
Where Borrower Data Goes
Employment, Income & Liabilities
All employment history, income details, and liability information is entered in the Application section — also referred to as the Mortgage Application or 1003 format. Not in the Client Profile, not in Notes, not in Mortgage Request.
Application section = income, employment, liabilities
Credit Bureau Pull
Before pulling a borrower's credit bureau in Velocity, written or digital borrower consent is required. A SIN card copy is not consent. Having a lender selected is not consent. Uploaded income documents are not consent. Consent is a standalone legal requirement.
No consent = no credit pull

Documents & Compliance

How Velocity handles document requests, uploads, and compliance form generation.

Document Portal
Requesting Documents from Clients
Use the Doc Request / Document Portal feature to request documents from clients in an organized, trackable way. This is not the eSign tool, not Billing, not Reports. The portal sends a structured request to the borrower and tracks what has been received vs. outstanding.
Doc Request/Portal = organized client document collection
Where Uploaded Documents Appear
When a client uploads documents through the Velocity portal, they appear in the Deal Documents section inside that deal file. They do not appear in Tasks, not in the Lender Submission area, not only in the client file. Review Deal Documents after each client upload.
Check Deal Documents after every client upload
Compliance Tab
The Compliance tab in Velocity is used to generate compliance forms — disclosure forms, consent documents, and other mandatory regulatory paperwork. It is not for marketing campaigns, not for referral agreements, and not for requesting appraisals. Any time a compliance form is needed, go to the Compliance tab first.
Compliance tab = disclosure and consent forms

Lender Submission Workflow

Steps for submitting through Velocity and what happens after submission.

Expert Submit — Pre-Submission Requirements
1
Complete All Mandatory Application Fields
Before submitting through Velocity's Expert Submit, all mandatory application fields must be completed. Assigning the deal to an agent, mapping documents, or uploading an appraisal report are not the first required step — mandatory fields completion is. Velocity will flag incomplete fields before allowing submission.
2
Add Notes to Lender
Notes to Lender provide additional context or explanation for the application — unusual income situations, compensating factors, or circumstances that need underwriter awareness. Do not use this field to message the borrower or create internal tasks. It goes directly to the lender's underwriter.
3
Confirm in the Submission Log
After a deal is successfully submitted, it appears in the Submission Log. The deal does not disappear from the Dashboard, the lender does not automatically approve it, and borrowers do not receive underwriting results instantly. Monitor the Submission Log to confirm submissions were received.

Communication & Permissions

How eSign works, why communication logging matters, and who controls system settings.

eSign Feature
What eSign Does in Velocity
Velocity's eSign feature is used to send documents for electronic signature. It is not for creating credit reports, scheduling appointments, or pulling income data. When a document needs to be signed by the borrower or other parties, use eSign to send it digitally and track when it has been signed.
Logging and Permissions
Why Communication Logging Matters
Every communication — calls, emails, messages — must be logged within the deal file in Velocity. This supports compliance requirements and maintains a full audit trail. If a dispute arises and the communication is not logged, it cannot be verified. Log every significant interaction immediately after it occurs.
Unlogged communication = no audit trail = compliance risk
Who Controls System Settings
Only Admin-level users can adjust system-wide settings and permissions in Velocity. Clients, referral partners, and regular agents cannot modify global settings. As a VA, if a permission or settings issue arises, escalate it to the broker or Admin — do not attempt to change settings yourself.
System settings = Admin only
