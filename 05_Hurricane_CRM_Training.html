CRM Basics

What Hurricane CRM is, what it stores, and how VAs use the dashboard.

What is Hurricane CRM
Primary Purpose
Hurricane CRM is a customer relationship management platform used to manage customer interactions, track leads, handle sales pipelines, and run support operations. For mortgage VAs, it provides structure for managing borrower and referral relationships, tracking opportunities, and ensuring no follow-up falls through the cracks.
CRM = track, manage, follow up, report
What is Stored in a Customer Profile
Customer profiles store: contact information (name, phone, email, address), purchase or interaction history, and support ticket history. Customer hobbies, personal preferences, and lifestyle data are not stored in a CRM profile and are not relevant to mortgage operations.
Store only relevant professional data
The Dashboard
The Hurricane CRM dashboard is your operational command center. It is used for viewing and managing leads and tasks — not for sending marketing emails automatically, running payroll, or managing physical inventory. When you log in, the dashboard shows your active leads, pending tasks, and pipeline status at a glance. Check it at the start of every shift.
Key Benefits of Using a CRM
Why CRM Matters
The primary benefit of a CRM system is improved customer engagement and sales efficiency. It keeps all customer data centralized, prevents lost follow-ups, and gives the broker visibility into pipeline activity. A well-maintained CRM is the difference between a reactive and a proactive mortgage operation.
CRM Analytics
Effective CRM analytics are powered by customer interactions and sales history — not office layout plans, employee vacation data, or weather forecasts. The data you enter into Hurricane CRM directly determines the quality of the insights the broker can draw from it. Accurate data entry is a VA's most important CRM responsibility.
Garbage data in = useless analytics out

Lead & Sales Management

How leads are handled, qualified, scored, and moved through the pipeline.

Lead Lifecycle
New Lead
Lead enters system — review and qualify immediately
Qualified
Lead shows interest and fits target profile — convert to opportunity
Opportunity
Active potential deal — manage through pipeline stages
Won / Lost
Closed successfully or decided not to proceed
Lead Handling Rules
First Action on a New Lead
When a new lead is received, the first action is to review and qualify the lead — not close it immediately, not assign it to marketing, not delete duplicates as a first step. Qualification determines if the lead has potential before any further resources are invested.
Review and qualify first — always
When a Lead Becomes an Opportunity
A lead becomes an opportunity when there is demonstrated interest or potential purchase intent from the contact. It is not based on time in the system (30 days is irrelevant), not when the lead score drops, and not when they unsubscribe. Demonstrated interest is the only trigger.
Lead Scoring
Lead Scoring is the Hurricane CRM feature that automatically prioritizes leads based on their engagement level and how well they match the target borrower profile. High engagement + strong profile fit = high-quality lead. Low or invalid contact data = low-quality lead. Use lead scores to prioritize your follow-up order.
Lead Scoring = automatic prioritization tool
High-Quality vs. Low-Quality Leads
A high-quality lead shows repeated interest and matches the target client profile. Low-quality leads include contacts who unsubscribed immediately, have invalid email addresses, or came from competitor client lists. Do not invest equal time in all leads — prioritize by quality and lead score.
Handling Unresponsive Leads
If a lead is unresponsive for 3 months, the correct action is to re-engage with a targeted follow-up campaign — not delete the lead, not immediately mark as lost, and not convert it to an opportunity without any renewed contact. Re-engagement preserves the relationship and gives the lead another chance to convert. Mark as lost only after re-engagement attempts fail.
Unresponsive does not mean lost — re-engage first

Customer Support Module

How support tickets work in Hurricane CRM — from creation to resolution.

Ticket Handling Process
First Step When a Ticket is Submitted
When a customer submits a support ticket, the first step is to assign priority and route it to the correct team. Do not close it immediately, do not convert it to a lead, and do not delete the customer profile. Routing ensures the right person handles it within the right timeframe.
Priority assignment + correct routing = step one
Standard Billing Issue Priority
A standard billing issue ticket is typically assigned Medium priority — not Critical (reserved for system outages or data loss), not High (reserved for urgent client-impacting issues), and not Low (reserved for minor informational requests). Use the priority matrix consistently.
Billing issues = Medium priority by default
Before Closing a Ticket
Before closing any support ticket, a resolution summary must be added. Do not delete customer data, do not convert to a sales opportunity, and do not archive the system. The resolution summary creates a record of what was done and how — it is required for every ticket before closure.
No resolution summary = ticket cannot be closed
Ticket Escalation
A support ticket should be escalated when it remains unresolved beyond the defined SLA (Service Level Agreement) time. Do not escalate tickets that are resolved immediately, do not escalate based on customer politeness, and do not escalate low-priority tickets simply because they have waited. Escalate based on SLA breach.
Escalate on SLA breach — not on client emotion
Tracking Repeated Issues
The Ticket History feature in Hurricane CRM allows VAs to identify repeated support issues across multiple customers. This is valuable for spotting systemic problems — if many customers are raising the same issue, it signals a process or product problem that needs broker attention. Check Ticket History when patterns emerge.
How CRM Automation Helps Support Teams
CRM automation in Hurricane helps by routing tickets to the right agent and sending automated follow-up emails at defined intervals. It does not automatically close tickets without review, predict weather, or eliminate all manual record-keeping. Automation handles routing and reminders — humans handle resolution.

Reporting & Analytics

What reports are available in Hurricane CRM and what they tell you.

Key Reports
Quarterly Sales Overview
The Quarterly Sales Overview report shows a salesperson's or team's performance over a quarter — number of deals closed, revenue generated, and pipeline activity. This is the report to reference when the broker asks about team performance for a specific quarter.
Conversion Rate Report
The conversion rate report shows the percentage of leads that became opportunities or closed sales. It does not track birthdays, count support tickets, or show employee attendance. Conversion rate is the key metric for measuring how effectively the team turns interest into closed business.
What CRM Analytics Can Do
Hurricane CRM analytics can: track conversion rates, predict sales trends, and analyze call transcripts. What it cannot do: control employee payroll. Analytics are for sales and customer insight — payroll is handled by a separate HR or accounting system entirely.
Analytics = sales insight. Not payroll control.
Why Tracking Interactions Matters
Tracking customer interactions over time helps optimize engagement and tailor marketing strategies to individual borrower behavior. It does not reduce electricity costs, eliminate the sales team, or guarantee zero negative reviews. The value is personalization and targeting — not operations savings.
What Dashboards Display
Hurricane CRM dashboards display key metrics, lead statuses, and sales progress — not weather forecasts, employee medical records, or building floor plans. Dashboards give the broker and VA a real-time operational overview. Keep your dashboard data clean and current so it accurately reflects pipeline reality.

Advanced Features & Best Practices

How CRM automation works and how to keep the system clean and reliable.

CRM Automation Capabilities
What CRM Automation Can Do
Hurricane CRM automation can do all of the following simultaneously: send personalized follow-up emails, automatically escalate tickets that breach SLA, and update opportunity stages based on defined rules. When a broker or VA sets up automation correctly, these three actions happen without manual intervention — freeing up time for higher-value tasks.
CRM Maintenance Best Practices
Three Non-Negotiable CRM Maintenance Rules
1. Clean duplicate and outdated records regularly. Duplicate contacts corrupt analytics and lead scores. If you spot a duplicate, flag it for cleanup — do not let it sit.

2. Keep customer data accurate and updated. Outdated phone numbers, old email addresses, and incorrect deal stages make the CRM unreliable. Update records immediately when information changes.

3. Train all staff on proper CRM usage. A CRM is only as good as the data entered into it. Inconsistent usage across team members creates gaps and errors. Follow the same processes consistently.
All three rules apply — not just one or two
