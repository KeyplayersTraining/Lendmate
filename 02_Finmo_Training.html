Platform Basics

What Finmo is, how it is structured, and the key navigation areas VAs use daily.

What is Finmo
Primary Purpose
Finmo is a mortgage application and submission platform used by Canadian mortgage brokers. It handles the full application workflow — from inviting a borrower to collecting documents to submitting to lenders. VAs use it to manage files, collect documents, and support submissions.
Not a CRM — it is a mortgage origination platform
Key Navigation Areas
Pipeline: Shows all active and in-progress applications. This is your main working view as a VA. Tasks: Tracks pending actions, follow-ups, and deadlines. Contacts: Borrower and referral partner records. Library: Templates and document resources.
Pipeline = your daily working tab
User Roles in Finmo
There are three main roles: Admin — full access including team settings, integrations, and permission management. Agent/Broker — manages applications and client communication. Referral Partner — limited view access. As a VA, you will typically operate under the Agent role or a custom VA role assigned by your broker. Only Admins can change team settings.
Never change team settings without broker authorization

Borrower Application Workflow

How borrowers enter the system and what information they complete.

Inviting a Borrower
How Borrower Invitations Work
When you invite a borrower through Finmo, they receive a personalized application link — not a PDF form, not an email with attachments. This link opens their own secure application portal where they complete their information directly. This reduces manual data entry errors and keeps everything in one place.
What Borrowers Complete
Required Application Sections
Personal details (name, DOB, SIN, address), employment and income information, assets and liabilities, and the property details. These are the core sections every mortgage application requires.
All four sections must be complete before submission
What is NOT Required
Non-mortgage-related information such as preferred property flooring material, interior design preferences, or lifestyle choices are not part of the application. If a borrower asks what to fill in, direct them to the four core sections only.
Keep borrowers focused on the four required sections
Credit Report Pull — Consent First
Before pulling a borrower's credit report in Finmo, written consent must be obtained. A driver's license, SIN card, or proof of employment is not a substitute for consent. Consent is a legal requirement under Canadian privacy law (PIPEDA). Pull credit only after consent is on file and the broker has confirmed it is time to do so.
No consent = no credit pull. No exceptions.

Documents & Smart Docs

How Finmo handles document collection and what happens after upload.

Finmo Smart Docs
What Smart Docs Does
Smart Docs automatically generates document requests based on the application data the borrower has entered. Instead of manually listing every required document, Finmo looks at the file and sends a targeted request — income docs for salaried borrowers, self-employment docs for business owners, etc. This saves time and reduces the chance of missing documents.
Smart Docs = automated, application-specific doc requests
Income Verification Documents
The most commonly requested income document is a pay stub or T4. Not an MLS listing, not a purchase agreement, not a void cheque (that is for banking, not income). When reviewing income documents, confirm the document type matches the income type entered in the application.
What Happens After Upload
When a borrower uploads a document, it appears in the Documents section for the broker or VA to review. Documents do not auto-send to the lender, they do not expire after 24 hours, and they are not hidden from the broker. Review every upload promptly.
Check the Documents section after each borrower upload

Lender Submission Workflow

What needs to happen before submitting, and how non-integrated lenders are handled.

Pre-Submission Steps
1
Map Documents to Lender Requirements
Before submitting, documents must be mapped to the lender's specific requirements inside Finmo. This ensures the lender receives the correctly labelled documents they need. Skipping this step results in incomplete submissions.
2
Add a Submission Note
A Submission Note gives the lender a summary or supporting rationale for the deal — explaining strengths, addressing weaknesses, or flagging unusual circumstances. The broker typically writes this, but the VA may be asked to draft it based on broker guidance.
3
Non-Integrated Lenders
If a lender is not integrated with Finmo, the application must be exported as a PDF and sent manually. Do not attempt to create a custom landing page or add co-borrowers as workarounds — export PDF is the correct step.

Compliance & Permissions

What information must be protected and who can do what in Finmo.

Data Confidentiality
Most Sensitive Data
Credit bureau details — including credit score, payment history, and outstanding debts — must be handled with the highest level of confidentiality. This data is legally protected. Never share, screenshot, or forward credit data outside of the platform without explicit broker authorization.
Credit data = highest confidentiality classification
Admin-Only Actions
Only an Admin-level user can edit team settings and permission configurations. As a VA, you can create borrower files, upload documents, and invite borrowers — but you cannot change who has access to what. If a permission issue arises, escalate to the broker.
Permission changes = broker/Admin only
Consent is a Legal Requirement
Canadian privacy law requires borrower consent before accessing credit data. This is not optional, not a recommendation, and not waivable under any circumstance. Consent must be on file before a credit pull. If you are ever in doubt, wait and ask the broker.
