The 4 Mortgage Transaction Types

Every mortgage file falls into one of three transaction types. Each has its own document focus. Confirming the transaction type is the first thing you do before collecting anything.

Transaction Types
🏡

Purchase

Buying a property with a new mortgage. Focuses on down payment sourcing and property details. Requires the most documentation.

🔁

Refinance

Restructuring an existing mortgage. Focuses on property equity and mortgage repayment history.

🔄

Switch / Transfer

Moving an existing mortgage to a new lender without changing the loan amount. Focuses on credit stability and payment history.

Purchase: Core Documents

Purchase files require the most documentation. These are the foundational documents that establish the transaction and verify the borrower's identity. Every purchase file needs all of these.

Essential Documents
1

Purchase & Sale Agreement (APS)

Must be fully signed by all parties before submission. All addendums must be attached.

  • Confirm all pages are included and all parties have signed
  • Check closing date, property address, and purchase price match the file
  • All schedule addendums (A, B, etc.) must be present
2

MLS Listing

Required for resale properties. Confirms property type, address, lot details, and specifications the lender needs to underwrite.

  • Property address must match exactly across all documents
  • For new construction, builder documents replace the MLS listing
3

Government-Issued Photo ID

Valid photo ID required for all borrowers on the file. Driver's license or passport.

  • Must not be expired
  • Name on ID must match name on all mortgage documents exactly
  • Both sides of driver's license are typically required
4

Real Estate Salesperson Info

Contact and licensing details for the real estate agent involved in the transaction. Required by the lender to verify the agent's registration.

  • Agent's full name and brokerage
  • RECO registration number (Ontario) or provincial equivalent
  • Contact phone and email
⚠️ Name match rule: The borrower's name across the ID, APS, and mortgage application must be identical. One discrepancy will generate a lender condition and delay the file.

Purchase: Income & Down Payment

After core documents, income verification and down payment sourcing are the two areas lenders scrutinize most on a purchase file. Requirements differ based on employment type.

Income Verification

💼 Employed Applicants

  • Pay stubs from the last 30 days
  • T4 slips for the past 1 to 2 years
  • Employment letter dated within 30 to 60 days confirming position, salary, and employment type

🗂 Self-Employed Applicants

  • T1 General forms for the last 2 years
  • Notice of Assessment (NOA) from CRA for last 2 years
  • Articles of Incorporation (if incorporated)
  • Master Business License (if sole proprietor)
Down Payment & Source of Funds
01

Proof of Down Payment + 90-Day Bank Statements

Documentation confirming the full amount is available. All accounts contributing must show a full 90-day transaction history. Lenders look for large unexplained deposits.

02

Gift Letter (if applicable)

Required if any portion of the down payment is a gift from a family member. Must be signed by the donor and confirm the funds are a gift, not a loan.

03

RRSP / Home Buyers' Plan (HBP) Documents

If the borrower is using their RRSP through the Home Buyers' Plan, withdrawal documents are required to confirm the amount and eligibility.

04

Sale Agreement (if applicable)

If down payment funds are coming from the sale of an existing property, the sale agreement and closing statement are required to trace the source of funds.

⚠️ Common VA error: Collecting only 30 days of bank statements for the down payment. The requirement is always 90 days for all accounts contributing to the down payment. Submitting less will generate a lender condition.

Refinance & Switch Documents

Refinance and Switch/Transfer share a similar document set. The focus shifts from proving a new purchase to documenting an existing mortgage and property. The key difference between them is purpose and whether the loan amount changes.

Core Documents (All Three Types)

📄 Mortgage Documents

  • Current mortgage statement showing balance, rate, and payment history
  • Charge/Mortgage document (original registered mortgage)
  • Transfer/Deed of the property
  • Payout statement from current lender (if available for Switch)

🏠 Property Documents

  • Property tax statement from municipality
  • Property insurance policy or binder
  • Mortgage repayment history (if applicable)
  • Property appraisal (if requested by lender)
Income Verification

📋 Same as Purchase When Income is Used for Qualification

  • Pay stubs (last 30 days) and T4 slips (last 1 to 2 years) for employed applicants
  • T1 General and NOA (last 2 years) for self-employed applicants
  • Employment letter if required by the lender
Key Differences Between the Two
Re

Refinance

Restructuring the mortgage. The loan amount may change. Focus is on property equity and mortgage history. Asset statements only needed if funds are being paid out.

Sw

Switch / Transfer

Loan amount does not change. Moving to a new lender only. Focus is on credit stability and payment record. Typically the lightest documentation load of the two.

ℹ️ For Switch/Transfer files, you do NOT need a Purchase & Sale Agreement, down payment proof, or 90-day bank statements. The loan amount is not changing, so source of funds verification is not required.

Common Documents: All File Types

Regardless of transaction type, every mortgage file requires this universal set of documents. Have these ready before submitting any application.

Universal Requirements

🪪 Government-Issued ID

  • Valid photo ID for all borrowers on the file
  • Must not be expired
  • Name must match all documents exactly
  • Both sides of driver's license if used

📊 Credit Bureau Report

  • Pulled by the lender via Equifax or TransUnion
  • Not provided by the borrower
  • Your role: ensure the signed consent form is in the file before credit is pulled

📝 Signed Application & Consent Forms

  • All lender-required forms must be signed before the file can be processed
  • Consent form must be signed before any credit inquiry
  • Use the lender's specific forms, not generic ones

📍 Proof of Address

  • May be requested depending on the file type and lender
  • Utility bill, bank statement, or official document showing current residential address
  • Check lender checklist to confirm if required
Key Focus Areas by Transaction Type
🏡

Purchase

Heavy focus on down payment sourcing. Every dollar must be traced with a clear paper trail. Highest documentation load.

🔁

Refinance

Emphasis on property equity and mortgage repayment history. Lenders assess how much equity exists and how reliably payments have been made.

🔄

Switch / Transfer

Focus on credit stability, payment history, and property value. Lenders want confidence the borrower is low-risk before taking on the file.

✅ Rule of thumb: When in doubt, collect more documentation upfront. It is always easier to have it and not need it than to chase it down mid-approval.

Quick Summary

  • ID + APS + MLS + Agent Info

  • 90-day bank statements + Down payment proof
  • Gift Letter / RRSP / Sale docs if applicable

  • Current mortgage statement + Charge doc + Transfer/Deed
  • Property tax statement + Insurance
  • Income documents

  • Property tax + Insurance binder
  • Payout statement if available
  • No down payment or APS needed

  • Credit bureau consent form
  • Signed application and lender forms
  • Proof of address (if requested)
