Down Payment Sources & Required Proof

Every single dollar of a down payment must have a documented, traceable source. No exceptions.

Acceptable Sources — What to Collect
💰 Personal Savings
90-day bank statement history — full PDF, all pages included, borrower's name visible on account, no date gaps anywhere in the 90 days
Most common source
🎁 Gift from Immediate Family
Signed gift letter (broker's template — not freeform) stating: amount is a gift, is NON-REPAYABLE at any time, donor is an immediate family member. PLUS bank proof of the transfer.
Both docs required — upload together
🏦 RRSP — Home Buyers' Plan (HBP)
Proof of RRSP withdrawal under the HBP program + withdrawal amount confirmed matches LOS entry. First-time buyers only. Max $35,000 per person / $70,000 per couple.
First-time buyer only
🏠 Sale of Existing Property
Sale agreement + final statement of adjustments. Label the file: "Sale Proceeds — [Property Address]". Closing date of existing property must precede the purchase closing date.
Label with address
📜 Inheritance
Letter of inheritance (from lawyer or estate) + bank deposit proof showing funds landing in borrower's account. Upload both documents together as a single package — never one without the other.
Never split into separate uploads
RRSP — Home Buyers' Plan Deep Dive
🏦 RRSP Home Buyers' Plan — Full Rules
Max Withdrawal
$35,000 per person. $70,000 for couples purchasing together — each withdraws up to $35,000 from their own RRSP.
Who Qualifies
First-time buyers only. CRA defines this as: has not owned a principal residence at any point during the 4 calendar years before the withdrawal year.
Repayment Rule
Must repay the full amount back to your RRSP within 15 years. It's a loan from yourself — not a grant. Failure to repay each year = that portion added to taxable income.
VA Action
Upload withdrawal confirmation. Confirm amount matches what's entered in the LOS. Note: HBP is NOT eligible for second homes or vacation properties — only principal residences.
Gift Letter — What Must Be In It
✓
Date at the top of the letter
✓
Dollar amount of the gift clearly stated
✓
Names of both the donor(s) and recipient(s) — printed and signed
✓
Property address the gift is being used toward
✓
Statement that the funds are a genuine gift and are NOT required to be repaid at any time
✓
Statement that no third party with interest in the sale provided the funds
✓
Confirmation that the donor is an immediate family member
✓
Signatures of both donor AND recipient — not just one
✗
If donor is NOT an immediate family member → this is a red flag. Flag to broker immediately.

Minimum Down Payment Rules & Math

Canada's sliding-scale minimum down payment rules — every VA must know these cold.

The Three Tiers
Purchase Price: $0 – $500,000
5%
of the full purchase price. Simple flat rate.
Purchase Price: $500,001 – $999,999
5% + 10%
5% on the first $500,000 PLUS 10% on every dollar above $500,000
Purchase Price: $1,000,000+
20%
Flat 20% minimum. No mortgage default insurance available at this price point — ever.

Example B — Split Calc

10% × $250,000 = $25,000
$25,000 + $25,000
Min Down Payment = $50,000
Example C — Over $1M
$1,200,000
Over $1M — flat 20%
20% × $1,200,000
No insurance available
Min Down Payment = $240,000

Why This Matters for the VA
When a client submits bank statements, the VA must confirm the total documented funds are equal to or greater than the minimum down payment for that purchase price. If the documented amount falls short — flag to broker BEFORE submitting the file. Do NOT calculate the LTV yourself or decide whether the file qualifies. That is the broker's job. Your job is to verify the paper trail is there and the total adds up.

Red Flags & FINTRAC

What to look for, what it means legally, and exactly how to escalate it.

What Is FINTRAC?
Financial Transactions & Reports Analysis Centre of Canada
What It Is
Canada's federal financial intelligence unit. Mandated to detect and prevent money laundering and terrorist financing. Reports to the Minister of Finance.
Why Brokerages Are Involved
Mortgage brokerages are legally "reporting entities" under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act. They must verify identity, keep records, and report suspicious activity.
The VA's Role
OBSERVE and REPORT only. You are the first set of eyes on documents. You flag red flags to the broker using neutral language. You do NOT accuse, delete, dismiss, or contact the client directly about a red flag.
The $10,000 Rule
Any single cash transaction of $10,000 or more MUST be reported to FINTRAC by the brokerage within 15 business days. This is a legal requirement — not optional. VA flags it → broker handles the report. VA never files a FINTRAC report.
KYC — Know Your Client: Source of Funds
KYC Requirement What It Means VA Task
Identity Verification Two government-issued IDs per borrower, or one ID + credit file match Collect IDs; confirm they are clear, unexpired, and names match all other docs
Address Verification Utility bill or bank statement showing current name and address Upload clearly labeled, within 90 days, name matches ID
Occupation Client's current job title or type of business Confirm it's entered accurately in the LOS application
Source of Funds Where the down payment money actually came from Ensure every source has documented, traceable proof in the file
Red Flags — Escalate Immediately to Broker
⚠
Large cash deposit with no explanation or paper trail — especially one that appears right before the purchase date. Even if it's a round number with no corresponding income deposit.
⚠
Gift funds visible in bank statement but no signed gift letter has been collected. Funds must match letter — amount in statement = amount in gift letter.
⚠
Funds transferred from a non-family member with no supporting explanation. FINTRAC requires family-only gifts — anyone else raises a flag regardless of intent.
⚠
Overseas transfer with no context, explanation, or supporting documentation. Especially flagged when the source country has no clear connection to the borrower.
⚠
Bank statement shows incomplete dates, unusual date sequences, or missing pages. Even a single missing page means the 90-day window is not confirmed.
⚠
Screenshot submitted instead of official bank-generated PDF statement. Screenshots can be edited. Request the full official PDF — no exceptions.
⚠
Down payment amount is dramatically out of line with the client's stated income, employment type, or age. Apply the "reasonableness test." If it doesn't make sense, flag it.
⚠
Documents appear edited — mismatched fonts, inconsistent spacing, cut-off fields, or pages that don't match the overall document format.
⚠
Bank account ownership includes unknown parties — name on the account does not match the borrower or is a joint account with an unexplained third party.
⚠
Bank statement deposits don't reflect income consistency — deposits are irregular, come from many different sources, or there are "even dollar amount" balances (a known fraud indicator).
⚠
Earnest/deposit money already paid to seller is not reflected as a withdrawal in the bank statements. If they paid a deposit, it should show as a debit in the account.
⚠
Client avoids or delays ID verification, or gives information inconsistent with other docs. VA flags this to broker — do not press the client directly.
The $10,000 Cash Reporting Rule — Detail
⚡ FINTRAC Large Cash Transaction Report (LCTR)
Any single cash transaction of $10,000 or more must be reported to FINTRAC by the brokerage within 15 business days. This is a federal legal requirement under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act — there is no discretion involved.

What triggers it: A single cash deposit of $10,000+, or two or more cash transactions that together total $10,000+ if the brokerage knows or suspects they are related.

VA action: If you spot a cash deposit of $10,000 or more in a client's bank statement, flag it to the broker immediately using neutral language. The broker determines if a FINTRAC LCTR report is required and files it. The VA does not file reports.

Note: "Structuring" — deliberately breaking large amounts into smaller deposits to avoid the $10,000 threshold — is itself a criminal offence under Canadian law. Multiple deposits of $9,000 that seem coordinated are just as much a flag as a single $10,000 deposit.
Consequences of Non-Compliance
What Happens If Red Flags Are Ignored
Brokerages that fail to detect and report suspicious activity face: administrative monetary penalties from FSRA, licence suspension or revocation, criminal charges under the MBLAA. A VA who ignores red flags and submits a file with fraud indicators puts the brokerage at risk of all of the above. Your job as a VA is to protect the broker by being the first line of defence — not to process files as fast as possible.
Escalation Script — Use Every Time. Always Stay Neutral.
"Hi [Broker Name], while reviewing [Client Name]'s bank statement, I noticed a [dollar amount] deposit on [date] with no visible source. Would you like me to request an explanation or supporting document from the client?"
❌ Never Say This
"I think the client is laundering money." — You never accuse. You observe and report. Stay completely neutral every time.

LTV & Mortgage Default Insurance

How the down payment percentage connects to LTV, insurance requirement, and premium cost.

Loan-to-Value (LTV) Formula
LTV = Loan Amount ÷ Property Value × 100
Loan Amount = Purchase Price − Down Payment

⚡ Critical Concept — Who Is Protected?
Mortgage default insurance protects the LENDER — not the borrower. The borrower pays the premium (added to the loan), but if they default and the lender loses money on the power of sale, the insurer reimburses the lender. The insurer then has the right to pursue the borrower for that amount. This is why CMHC, Sagen, or Canada Guaranty sometimes appear as creditors in bankruptcy proceedings.

💡 VA Accuracy Warning
A simple data entry error in the LOS — entering the wrong purchase price or down payment amount — can shift the LTV and change whether the file is insured, insurable, or uninsurable. This is not a cosmetic error. It can affect the client's interest rate, the lender's product offering, and whether the deal closes. Double-check every number you enter against the source documents.

VA Workflow — Down Payment Verification

Step-by-step process from receiving the file to handing off to broker.

Step-by-Step Process
1
Identify the Down Payment Source(s) in the LOS
Open the LOS application and locate what the broker entered as source of funds. There may be multiple sources (e.g. personal savings + partial gift). Note each source — you'll need to collect documents for all of them.
2
Collect the Correct Documents for Each Source
Match each source to its required proof:
Savings → 90-day bank statement (full PDF, not screenshot)
Gift → Signed gift letter (broker's template) + bank proof of transfer
RRSP/HBP → Withdrawal confirmation with amount
Sale of property → Sale agreement + statement of adjustments
Inheritance → Inheritance letter + bank deposit confirmation

Never accept one document when two are required. Never accept a screenshot for a bank statement.
3
Review Each Document — Run Your Checks
For bank statements: confirm 90 days covered with no gaps, all pages present, borrower's name matches, no large unexplained deposits, full PDF format. For gift letters: signed by both parties, non-repayable language present, donor is immediate family, bank transfer proof attached. For all docs: look for editing signs — mismatched fonts, spacing anomalies, cut-off fields.
4
Confirm Total Documented Funds ≥ Minimum Down Payment
Add up all documented sources. The total must meet or exceed the minimum down payment for that purchase price. If it falls short — flag to broker before uploading. Do not submit an incomplete file and hope the broker catches it.
5
Name & File Each Document Correctly
File naming format: YYYY-MM-DD_DocumentType_ClientName.pdf
✓ GOOD: 2025-05-01_BankStatement_RBC_90Days_JuanDelaCruz.pdf
✓ GOOD: 2025-05-02_GiftLetter_Parents_signed_MariaReyes.pdf
✓ GOOD: 2025-05-03_RRSPWithdrawal_HBP_JuanDelaCruz.pdf
✓ GOOD: 2025-05-01_SaleProceeds_123MainSt_AnaReyes.pdf

✗ BAD: screenshot.png
✗ BAD: doc(1).pdf
✗ BAD: Marias bank stuff.pdf

Folder: Assets & Down Payment (never in Income or other folders)
6
Flag Any Red Flags to Broker — Before Moving Forward
If any red flag is present — stop. Do not upload the file and move on. Flag to broker using neutral language before anything else happens. Never contact the client directly about a red flag.
7
Final Quality Check Before Handing to Broker
Before submitting: All sources documented? All files named per convention? No missing pages? No screenshots? Folder correctly labelled? All amounts match LOS entries? Treat every file as if it will be audited tomorrow — because it might be.
What the VA Does vs. Does Not Do
✓ VA Does
Collect and verify documents for each down payment source
Check 90-day coverage, all pages, borrower name on bank statements
Verify gift letter has all required elements and both signatures
Name and file all docs correctly in the Assets & Down Payment folder
Confirm total documented funds meet the minimum DP requirement
Flag every red flag to broker using neutral language before proceeding
Enter data accurately into the LOS — numbers must match source docs
Treat every file like it will be audited
✗ VA Does NOT
Calculate LTV or decide if the file qualifies for a mortgage
Choose or recommend a mortgage insurer (CMHC / Sagen / Canada Guaranty)
Contact the client directly about a red flag — ever
Accept screenshots as bank statements — full PDF only
File a FINTRAC Large Cash Transaction Report (that's the broker)
Accept a gift letter from a non-family member without flagging it
Submit a file with a known red flag hoping someone else catches it
Decide whether mortgage default insurance applies — that's the broker
File Folder Reminder
📁 Assets & Down Payment → Bank statements · Gift letters · RRSP withdrawals · Sale proceeds · Inheritance docs
📁 Income → Pay stubs (ADP, May statements) · Employment letters · T4s · NOAs — NOT here for down payment docs
Escalation Script — Memorize This. Use It Every Time.
"Hi [Broker Name], while reviewing [Client Name]'s bank statement, I noticed a [dollar amount] deposit on [date] with no visible source. Would you like me to request an explanation or supporting document from the client?"
