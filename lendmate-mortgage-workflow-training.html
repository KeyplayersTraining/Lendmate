Introduction

Your Role as a Mortgage VA

Understand what you are here to do — and what is strictly off-limits.

As a Mortgage VA, your role is to support the broker and their clients throughout the entire mortgage process. While you cannot provide advice or make lending decisions, you are a crucial part of the admin, document management, and client communication workflow. Every agent or broker does things a little differently — but this overview gives you a solid understanding of the typical mortgage process.

Your Core Functions

✅ What You Do

  • Administrative support
  • Document management and tracking
  • Client communication (as directed by broker)
  • Process coordination
  • CRM updates and file organization
  • Status updates and follow-ups

❌ What You Cannot Do

  • Provide mortgage advice to clients
  • Make lending decisions
  • Pull credit reports
  • Negotiate mortgage terms
  • Give opinions on which lender to use
  • Access client financial data unsupervised
⚠️ Critical boundary: If a client asks you a question about rates, qualifications, or mortgage advice, your answer is always: "I will pass that along to your broker who will follow up with you shortly." Never attempt to answer mortgage-specific questions yourself.

Stage 1 of 9

Application Stage

The mortgage process starts here. Accuracy at this stage prevents problems at every stage after it.

The broker sends out a borrower application form via platforms like Finmo, Velocity, or Filogix. The client completes it with personal info, employment details, assets, and liabilities, and provides consent for a credit check. Your job is to make sure nothing is missing before the file moves forward.

Broker's Actions at This Stage
→

Sends Application Form

Sends borrower application via Finmo, Velocity, Filogix, or similar platforms.

→

Client Completes Application

Client fills in personal info, employment details, assets, and liabilities.

→

Obtains Credit Consent

Broker obtains the client's signed consent for a credit check before proceeding.

Your VA Tasks
✓

Review for Completeness

Check all required fields are filled in. Flag any missing employer names, addresses, or other key information before the broker reviews.

✓

Upload to CRM

Upload the completed application into the CRM system and organize the borrower's profile with correct folder structure.

✓

Create Borrower Profile

Set up the borrower's file in the system. Ensure contact details, property address, and file reference numbers are correctly entered.

💻 Key Platforms: Familiarize yourself with Finmo, Velocity, and Filogix. These are the primary tools brokers use for application management. Your broker will tell you which one they use.
Stage 2 of 9

Document Collection

The foundation of every mortgage. Document quality directly impacts approval speed.

Document collection is where accuracy and organization become critical. The broker requests supporting documents based on borrower type (salaried, self-employed, etc.) and needs accurate documents to submit to lenders. Your role covers collection, tracking, and quality control.

The 3-Step Collection Process
📋

01 Broker Requests

Broker requests supporting documents based on borrower type. Needs accurate docs to submit to lenders.

📥

02 VA Collects & Tracks

Collect and track borrower documents, ensuring all materials are legible, recent, and complete.

🗂

03 Organize & QC

Organize files into correct folders in broker's system and flag discrepancies without confronting client.

Essential Documents Checklist

💼 Employed Borrowers

  • 2 recent pay stubs (last 30 days)
  • T4 slips for the last 1 to 2 years
  • Employment letter (dated within 30 to 60 days)
  • Government-issued photo ID (both sides)
  • NOA (Notice of Assessment) from CRA
  • Bank statements (last 90 days)

🗂 Self-Employed Borrowers

  • T1 General (last 2 years)
  • NOA (Notice of Assessment) from CRA
  • Articles of Incorporation or Business License
  • Bank statements (last 90 days)
  • Government-issued photo ID (both sides)
  • Any additional borrower-specific documentation
VA Quality Standards
✓

Check Legibility

Every document must be clearly readable. If a scan is too dark, blurry, or cut off, request a new copy before uploading.

✓

Check Currency

Pay stubs must be within 30 days. Employment letters within 30 to 60 days. Bank statements within 90 days. Flag anything outside these windows.

✓

Flag Discrepancies Correctly

If something does not match (name spelling, address, employer name), do not confront the client. Flag it to the broker with a specific note in the file.

⚠️ Common error: Uploading documents before checking they are complete and legible. A blurry or cut-off document will be rejected by the lender and come back as a condition, causing delays.
Stage 3 of 9

Credit Check

The broker pulls credit. Your role here is purely administrative.

The broker runs the credit bureau through Equifax or TransUnion via the mortgage platform. They match the credit report with liabilities listed in the application and analyze credit score and payment history. This is a broker-only function. Your role is secure storage and cross-referencing.

Broker Responsibilities (Not Your Job)

🔒 Broker Does This — Not You

  • Runs credit bureau through Equifax or TransUnion via mortgage platform
  • Matches credit report with liabilities listed in application
  • Analyzes credit score and payment history
  • Makes decisions based on the credit report
Your VA Critical Tasks
!

Never Pull Credit Yourself

Credit pulls can only be performed by licensed mortgage professionals. If you are ever asked to pull a credit report, decline and notify the broker immediately. This is a legal boundary.

✓

Secure Storage

Ensure the credit report is uploaded and stored securely in the correct folder in the broker's system. Access should be restricted to authorized personnel only.

✓

Cross-Check Liabilities

Cross-check the liabilities listed in the file (car loans, credit cards, student loans, etc.) to confirm they match what the borrower disclosed in the application. Flag any discrepancies to the broker.

🚫 Important: Credit pulls can only be performed by licensed mortgage professionals. Your role is purely administrative — secure storage and cross-referencing existing information. Never attempt to access credit bureau systems.
Stage 4 of 9

Lender Search & Submission

The broker selects the lender. You prepare and verify the submission package.

The broker compares different lender products including rates, terms, and conditions to find the best fit. Once selected, a comprehensive mortgage submission package is prepared and submitted through the appropriate lender portal. Your role is to support package preparation and verification.

Broker's Actions (Context for You)
🔍

01 Lender Comparison

Broker compares lender products including rates, terms, and conditions to find the best fit for the borrower.

📦

02 Package Preparation

Broker selects the most suitable lender and prepares the comprehensive mortgage submission package.

📤

03 Submission

Complete package is submitted through the appropriate lender portal or system.

Your VA Tasks
✓

Format & Label Documents

Format documents clearly and label them for easy identification and processing.

  • Use consistent naming: e.g., "T4_2023.pdf", "PayStub_Mar2025.pdf"
  • Group by category: Income, ID, Property, Application
  • Remove duplicate or outdated versions from the folder
✓

Package Verification

Double-check submission package completeness before the broker submits. Confirm these are all present:

  • Completed application form
  • Credit bureau consent (signed)
  • Income documents (pay stubs, T4s, NOA)
  • Down payment proof (for purchase files)
  • Property details / MLS listing
✓

Portal Upload

Upload the package into the lender portal when directed by the broker, ensuring all documents are properly categorized. Never submit without explicit broker direction.

💡 You assist with document preparation and upload — but the broker decides which lender to use and makes the submission decision. Never select a lender or submit independently.

Stage 5 of 9

Conditional Approval ("Commitment Letter")

A major milestone — but conditions must be satisfied before final approval.

Receiving a commitment letter is a major milestone. The lender reviews the submission package and issues a conditional approval letter listing what still needs to be satisfied — commonly things like an employment letter, updated bank statements, or a property appraisal. The broker discusses these conditions with the borrower. Your job is to track and action every single condition.

What the Lender Issues
📄

Commitment Letter

The lender's conditional approval. Lists all conditions that must be satisfied before they will fund the mortgage. Read it carefully.

📋

Condition List

Typically includes items like: updated employment letter, bank statements, property appraisal, signed commitment by borrower, and any borrower-specific conditions.

Your VA Tasks
📊

Track Conditions

Maintain a detailed checklist of all lender conditions and their current status. Update it every time something is received or submitted.

📥

Document Collection

Request and collect additional documents from the borrower as specified in the conditions list. Follow up promptly.

🔔

Status Updates

Keep the broker informed on condition fulfillment progress and any challenges or delays as they arise.

✅ Best practice: As soon as you receive the commitment letter, create a condition tracking sheet. List every condition, the document needed, the date requested from borrower, and the date received. Share this with the broker daily.
Stage 6 of 9

Fulfillment of Conditions

All conditions must be satisfied. The lender then issues "file complete" status.

The broker ensures all lender conditions are met and prepares a resubmission package with additional documentation. The lender reviews completed conditions and issues "file complete" status once satisfied. This is your most active tracking and follow-up stage.

The Fulfillment Flow
1

Condition Satisfaction

Broker ensures all lender conditions are met and prepares resubmission package with additional documentation.

2

Lender Review

Lender reviews completed conditions and additional documents to verify compliance with requirements.

3

File Complete Status

Lender issues "file complete" status once satisfied with all conditions and documentation. This triggers the next stage.

Your VA Tasks
✓

Follow Up with Borrower

Maintain regular contact with the borrower for any outstanding documents or information. Be specific about what is needed and by when.

✓

Organize Condition Documents

Upload and organize all condition documents in appropriate system folders immediately upon receipt. Label clearly using the condition name.

✓

Verify Lender Checkboxes

Confirm all lender checkboxes are satisfied in the mortgage platform system before the broker resubmits. Do not assume — check each one explicitly.

✓

Update Condition Tracker

Update your condition tracking sheet daily. Mark items as received, pending, or still outstanding. Share updated status with broker at each check-in.

💡 When "file complete" status is issued by the lender, notify the broker immediately. This is the trigger for Stage 7 — File Sent to Lawyer.
Stage 7 of 9

File Sent to Lawyer

Once "file complete" is issued, the mortgage moves to legal processing.

Once the lender issues "file complete" status, the mortgage file is ready for legal processing and closing preparation. Detailed instructions are sent to the borrower's lawyer or notary public for final document preparation. Your role is to ensure the package reaches the right lawyer with everything included, and that receipt is confirmed.

What Happens at This Stage
📄

File Complete Issued

Lender issues "file complete" status. The mortgage file is now ready for legal processing and closing preparation. This is the trigger for this stage.

⚖️

Instructions to Lawyer

Detailed instructions are sent to the borrower's lawyer or notary public for final document preparation — mortgage registration, title search, and disbursement calculations.

📋

Legal Document Prep

Lawyer prepares final closing documents. This is managed by the lawyer, not the broker or VA — but you ensure they have everything they need.

Your VA Tasks
✓

Package Delivery

Send the complete package to the lawyer when directed by the broker. Ensure all documents are included and properly organized before sending.

✓

Contact Verification

Confirm the lawyer's contact details are accurate and update CRM with current information for seamless communication between broker and lawyer.

✓

Receipt Tracking

Track and confirm when the lawyer acknowledges receipt of the package and reviews contents for completeness. Log the date and time of confirmation in the file.

⚠️ Never send the package to the lawyer without the broker's explicit direction. Always double-check the lawyer's contact details match the file — sending to the wrong lawyer causes serious delays.
Stage 8 of 9

Closing

The culmination of weeks or months of preparation. Your job is clean file closure.

Closing day is when all the hard work pays off. The lawyer coordinates all final details — signing, title transfer, and funding — while the broker confirms everything proceeds smoothly. Your role at closing is minimal in real-time but critical in terms of file completion and post-closing admin.

Who Does What at Closing
⚖️

Lawyer

Manages signing, title transfer, and funding processes. Coordinates all legal aspects of the transaction.

🤝

Broker

Confirms closing completed successfully and follows up with client. Ensures client is satisfied.

🖥

VA (You)

Mark file closed in CRM, archive all documents, and set up post-closing tasks for the client.

Your VA Closing Tasks
✓

Mark File Closed

Update the CRM system to reflect the completed transaction status and close the active file. This keeps the pipeline clean and accurate.

✓

Archive Documents

Properly organize and archive all transaction documents according to compliance requirements. Documents must be stored and accessible for audit purposes.

✓

Post-Closing Tasks

Send a thank-you email on behalf of the broker, request a testimonial from the client, and move them into post-close nurture campaigns in the CRM.

Stage 9 of 9

Post-Close

Building long-term success through renewals and referrals.

The post-close phase is where lasting relationships are built. Your role in maintaining client connections directly contributes to the broker's long-term success through renewals and referrals. Clients who stay engaged post-close have an 80% renewal rate and generate an average of 3x referral potential.

Your Post-Close VA Tasks
✓

Database Management

Add the client to the newsletter list and maintain updated contact information in the CRM. Ensure their profile is tagged correctly for future outreach.

✓

Follow-Up Scheduling

Schedule 6-month check-ins and annual mortgage reviews in the CRM calendar. Set automated reminders so no client falls through the cracks.

✓

Renewal Preparation

Track renewal dates for all clients and prepare renewal outreach 3 to 4 months before the mortgage term ends. Renewals are a major revenue opportunity for the broker.

✓

Relationship Maintenance

Support the broker's client retention and referral strategies. This includes birthday messages, holiday greetings, and market update emails as directed by the broker.

✓

Referral Programs

Implement systems to encourage and track client referrals. When a client refers someone, ensure the referral is logged and the referring client is acknowledged.

✅ Remember: Your attention to detail and client care throughout the entire mortgage process directly impacts the broker's reputation and future business success. Every interaction matters.
