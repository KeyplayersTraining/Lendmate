    What is an eSign Package?

    An eSign package is a digital set of documents sent to borrowers, brokers, or third parties for electronic review and signature. It uses secure platforms like DocuSign or SignNow. As a LendMate VA, you will be responsible for preparing and managing these packages.

    Definition

    An eSign package replaces physical document signing. Instead of printing, couriering, and waiting for wet signatures, the entire process is done digitally. This speeds up mortgage processing significantly and reduces the risk of lost documents.

    ℹ️ In Canadian mortgage transactions, the eSign package is sent after the lender issues the Mortgage Commitment Letter. This is one of your core responsibilities as a VA.

    Common Documents in an eSign Package

    📋 Core Documents

    • Mortgage Commitment Letter
    • Mortgage Disclosure / Cost of Borrowing
    • PAD (Pre-Authorized Debit) Form
    • ID Verification Forms

    📎 Supporting Documents

    • Insurance Acknowledgements
    • Condo / Property Specific Disclosures
    • Borrower Declarations
    • Province-specific forms
    ⚠️ Important: The exact documents vary per lender and per province. Always confirm with your lender's checklist before assembling the package.

    Platforms Used

    🖊 DocuSign

    • Most commonly used by major lenders
    • Audit trail built-in
    • Email-based signing flow

    🖊 SignNow

    • Used by some brokers and smaller lenders
    • Similar functionality to DocuSign
    • Mobile-friendly for borrowers

    The Standard eSign Workflow

    Every eSign package follows the same six steps. This applies across all provinces. Skipping or rushing any step increases the risk of errors, lender rejection, and delays for the borrower.

    The 6 Steps

    1

    Prepare

    Gather and assemble all required documents based on lender requirements and the mortgage commitment letter.

    2

    Quality Control (QC)

    Review every document before uploading. Check names, terms, signatures fields, and province-specific forms.

    3

    Upload

    Upload all documents into the eSign platform in the correct signing order. Assign signer roles properly.

    4

    Send

    Send to the verified borrower email address with clear instructions and a stated completion deadline.

    5

    Monitor

    Track signing status daily. Follow up on Day 2-3 if not started. Escalate on Day 4+.

    6

    Complete & Download

    Download the fully executed package. Verify all signatures and timestamps, then save in the file management system.

    💡 Think of these six steps as a chain. Each one depends on the one before it. A mistake in Step 1 will still cause a problem at Step 6 even if Steps 2-5 were done correctly.
    Lesson 3 of 6

    Steps 1 & 2: Prepare & Quality Control

    The first two steps are where most errors originate. This is your foundation. If preparation and QC are done correctly, the rest of the process will be smooth.

    Step 1: Prepare Documents

    ✔ What to Confirm

    • Lender requirements and all conditions
    • Borrower names match ID exactly (no nicknames)
    • Property address is correct and complete
    • All financial terms are accurate

    💰 Financial Terms to Check

    • Mortgage rate (fixed or variable)
    • Amortization period (e.g., 25 years)
    • Term length (e.g., 5 years)
    • Payment frequency (monthly, bi-weekly, etc.)

    Step 2: Quality Control (QC)

    ✓

    Check Signature & Initial Fields

    Confirm no signature or initial fields are missing from any document. Every required field must have a tag in the eSign platform.

    ✓

    Verify All Exhibits & Attachments

    All supporting documents must be included. A missing exhibit can cause lender rejection of the entire package.

    ✓

    Validate Legal Names & Spelling

    The borrower's name must match their government-issued ID exactly. One letter difference can invalidate the signing.

    ✓

    Confirm Province-Specific Forms

    Ontario, Alberta, and BC each have different required disclosure forms. Using the wrong province's form is a compliance error.

    ⚠️ Golden Rule: Never skip QC, even if you are under time pressure. A five-minute check now prevents a 48-hour delay later.
    Lesson 4 of 6

    Steps 3-5: Upload, Send & Monitor

    Once QC is complete, you move into execution. Uploading correctly, sending to the right address, and actively monitoring are all critical to keeping the transaction on track.

    Step 3: Upload to eSign Platform

    3a

    Upload in Correct Signing Order

    Documents must appear in a logical order for the borrower. Typically: Commitment Letter first, then disclosures, then forms.

    3b

    Set Signer Roles Correctly

    Each signer type has a specific role:

    • Borrower (primary)
    • Co-Borrower (if applicable)
    • Guarantor (if applicable)
    • Witness (only required for specific documents)
    3c

    Add Signing Instructions

    Include clear, simple instructions for each recipient so they know exactly what to do and in what order.

    Step 4: Send the Package

    Use the standardized LendMate email template. Personalize the borrower's name and always state the completion deadline.

    Subject: Mortgage Documents for Electronic Signature
    Dear [Borrower Name],

    Please find your mortgage documents attached for electronic signature. Kindly review all documents carefully and complete the signing process by [Deadline Date].

    If you have any questions, please let us know.

    Thank you,
    [Your Name]
    ⚠️ Always confirm the borrower's email address before sending. An incorrect address creates audit trail gaps and delays the transaction. Double-check against the file, not just your memory.

    Step 5: Monitor & Follow Up

    D1

    Day 1 - Send + Confirm

    Send the package and a confirmation email. Log the send date in the file.

    D3

    Day 2-3 - Reminder

    If signing has not started, send a polite reminder. Check the platform for status.

    D4

    Day 4+ - Escalate

    Send a follow-up call or escalation email. Notify your broker or supervisor if the borrower is unresponsive.

    Common Errors & Best Practices

    These are the four most common errors VAs make when handling eSign packages. Knowing them is the first step to avoiding them.

    4 Errors to Avoid

    ❌ Name Mismatch

    Borrower spelling in the documents does not match their government-issued ID. This can invalidate the entire signing.

    ❌ Missing Initials

    Required initial fields on disclosure pages are left blank. Lenders will reject the package.

    ❌ Wrong Province Forms

    Using Ontario disclosure forms for a BC transaction, or vice versa. Each province has its own required documents.

    ❌ Incorrect Terms

    Wrong mortgage rate, wrong term length, or missing condo/strata documents for a condo property.

    Best Practices

    ★

    Cross-check commitment letter vs. lender instructions

    Never assume the lender's package template is fully complete. Always verify it matches the commitment letter terms.

    ★

    Use a checklist before every send

    Do not rely on memory. Use a QC checklist for every single package, regardless of how routine the file seems.

    ★

    Keep an audit trail

    Log every action: when you sent, when you followed up, when the borrower signed, when you downloaded. This protects everyone.

    ★

    Standardize folder naming

    Use consistent folder names so anyone on the team can find the completed package immediately. Example: [LastName_FirstName_YYYY-MM]

    Completion Checklist

    ✓
    All signatures present on every document
    ✓
    All initials completed on required pages
    ✓
    All documents downloaded from eSign platform
    ✓
    Final package saved in file management system
    ✓
    Lender conditions updated to reflect completion
