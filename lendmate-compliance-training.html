What Is a Compliance Package?

The official record of how the mortgage was approved, disclosed, and supported.

A compliance package is the complete documentation set required to demonstrate that a mortgage transaction meets all applicable standards. It is the official record of how the mortgage was approved, disclosed, and supported — and it must be ready for review at any time by regulators, lenders, or auditors.

Why It Exists

🏛 Federal Regulations

  • PIPEDA privacy requirements must be documented and signed
  • AML (Anti-Money Laundering) obligations under FINTRAC must be met
  • Applies to every mortgage file regardless of province

📋 Provincial Rules

  • Province-specific mortgage brokerage regulations vary
  • Forms, disclosures, and timelines differ by province
  • Your broker's compliance team sets province-specific requirements

🏦 Lender Requirements

  • Underwriting and disclosure standards set by each lender
  • Package must meet lender's specific checklist to avoid conditions
  • Non-compliance results in file rejection or delays

🔍 Internal Audit

  • Brokerage standards for audit readiness at all times
  • Every file must be organized, complete, and traceable
  • Gaps in documentation expose the brokerage to regulatory risk
Key Terms You Need to Know
TermWhat It Means for You
PIPEDAPersonal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act. Requires signed privacy consent from every borrower before their information is used.
FINTRAC / AMLFinancial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada. Anti-Money Laundering declarations must be completed on every file.
Audit-ReadyEvery document is present, consistent, signed, and traceable. A file can be pulled for review at any time without scrambling to find anything.
DisclosureThe borrower has been informed of all mortgage terms and has acknowledged understanding. This must be documented.
💡 A compliance package is not just paperwork. It is the legal defence of the entire mortgage transaction. If a document is missing, the file is incomplete — regardless of how smoothly the deal closed.

Core Components: Client & Financial Documents

Every compliance package starts with verified identity and confirmed income.

The first two document categories form the foundation of the compliance package. Without verified identity and confirmed income, nothing else in the file can be validated. These documents must be current, consistent, and match each other exactly.

Category 1: Client Identification & Consent
🪪

Government-Issued Photo ID (Verified)

Driver's license or passport. Must be valid (not expired). The name on ID must match the name on every other document in the file exactly. No nicknames, no initials only.

  • Both sides of driver's license required
  • Passport photo page for passports
  • VA must confirm expiry date is valid
✍

Credit Bureau Authorization (Signed)

The borrower's signed consent allowing the lender to pull their credit report. Must be obtained before any credit inquiry is made. This is a legal requirement, not optional.

📋

Mortgage Application (Full and Consistent)

The completed application form. Every field must be filled in. Information must be consistent with all supporting documents — employment, income, and liability information must match.

🔒

Privacy Consent (PIPEDA Compliance)

Signed consent authorizing collection, use, and disclosure of the borrower's personal information. Required under federal law. Must be in the file before any data is processed.

Category 2: Income & Financial Verification

💼 Employed Borrowers

  • Pay stubs (recent and consistent with application)
  • T4 slips for the last 1 to 2 years
  • Employment verification letter (if required by lender)
  • Bank statements for asset verification (last 90 days)

🗂 Self-Employed Borrowers

  • T1 General for the last 2 years
  • Notice of Assessment (NOA) from CRA
  • Business registration documents
  • Bank statements for asset verification (last 90 days)
⚠️ Consistency check: The income shown on pay stubs must match the income stated on the application. Any discrepancy — even a small one — will generate a lender condition and delay the file.

Core Components: Property, Disclosure & Regulatory Forms

Three more document categories that complete the compliance package.

Once identity and income are confirmed, the compliance package requires property documentation, mortgage disclosures, and regulatory compliance forms. These three categories make the transaction legally defensible and confirm the borrower has been properly informed.

Category 3: Property & Transaction

🏠 Required Property Documents

  • Purchase and sale agreement (APS) — fully signed by all parties with all addendums
  • MLS listing (if applicable) — for resale properties
  • Property tax statement — confirms current tax status
  • Insurance confirmation — proof of active home insurance with lender as additional insured
Category 4: Mortgage Disclosures

📄 Required Disclosure Documents

  • Commitment letter — the lender's conditional approval with all conditions listed
  • Cost of borrowing disclosure — total interest and fees over the mortgage term
  • Rate, term, and amortization disclosure — all mortgage terms clearly stated
  • Payment schedule and variable rate explanation (if applicable)
Category 5: Compliance & Regulatory Forms
📜

Brokerage Disclosure of Services & Compensation

Discloses to the borrower how the broker is compensated and what services they provide. Mandatory in all provinces. Must be signed before any mortgage advice is given.

⚖️

Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Discloses any relationship between the broker and the lender that could influence recommendations. Required by law in all provinces.

🔍

AML / FINTRAC Declarations

Anti-Money Laundering declarations required under federal law. Every borrower must complete these. Missing FINTRAC declarations can result in regulatory fines for the brokerage.

✅

Borrower Acknowledgment Forms

Confirms the borrower has received, reviewed, and understood all disclosures. These must be signed — not just presented. Unsigned acknowledgments are non-compliant.

🚫 Non-negotiable: AML/FINTRAC declarations and borrower acknowledgment forms must be present and signed in every file. Missing these two items is a direct regulatory compliance failure.

The 6-Step Compliance Workflow

Every file must follow this process from intake through final submission.

Every file must follow this six-step process to ensure completeness, accuracy, and audit readiness from intake through final submission. Each step builds on the last. Skipping or rushing any phase increases the risk of compliance errors, lender rejections, or audit failures.

The 6 Steps at a Glance
1

Intake Review

Verify completeness and consistency

2

Document Collection

Gather, validate, and confirm currency

3

Disclosure Verify

Confirm terms match and are disclosed

4

Checklist

All forms signed and complete

5

Final QC

Cross-check numbers and names

Steps 1 to 3: Intake to Disclosure
1

File Intake Review

Confirm application completeness. Verify borrower identity matches all documents and that property and mortgage details are consistent throughout the file.

  • Check all fields on the application are completed
  • Confirm borrower name matches ID exactly
  • Verify property address is consistent across all documents
2

Document Collection

Request missing documentation from the borrower or third parties. Confirm documents are current (generally within 90 days) and validate authenticity — no edits or inconsistencies.

  • Pay stubs and bank statements within 90 days
  • Employment letters dated within 30 to 60 days
  • Flag any document that appears altered — do not accept it
3

Disclosure Verification

Confirm mortgage terms match the approval sheet. Verify all disclosures are complete and accurate, and ensure borrower understanding is documented.

  • Rate, term, and amortization in disclosures must match commitment letter
  • Cost of borrowing disclosure must be signed by borrower
  • Borrower acknowledgment must confirm they have read and understood disclosures
Steps 4 to 6: Checklist to Submission
4

Checklist Completion

Confirm all required forms are included. Ensure all signatures and initials are completed and verify any province-specific requirements are met.

  • Work through the compliance checklist line by line
  • Do not mark anything complete unless you have physically verified it
  • Check that every signature field has been signed — not just initialled if a signature is required
5

Final QC Review

Cross-check all numbers — rate, payment, amortization. Confirm legal names match ID exactly and ensure no missing pages or attachments remain.

  • Compare rate and payment in the disclosure against the commitment letter
  • Confirm every attachment referenced in a document is actually present in the file
  • Check page count on multi-page documents — missing pages are a common failure point
6

Submission & Filing

Upload to lender/compliance system. Save the full package in the internal audit folder and record the submission and completion date.

  • Upload only when broker has confirmed the file is ready
  • Save a copy to the internal audit folder immediately after submission
  • Log the date and time of submission in the file notes
✅ Rule: Never skip a step because the file "looks complete." Use the checklist every time for every file. The checklist is your protection against errors.

Provincial Compliance: Key Principles

Core compliance principles that apply in every province across Canada.

While core requirements are consistent across Canada, each province has specific regulatory emphasis and documentation expectations. All brokers and VA staff must ensure compliance with the following five principles in every province. These are non-negotiable across all files.

The 5 Universal Compliance Principles
📢

Full Disclosure

Accurate disclosure of all mortgage terms to the borrower. The borrower must receive and sign disclosures covering the rate, term, amortization, payment schedule, and cost of borrowing before they commit to the mortgage.

🪪

Identity & Consent

Clear documentation of borrower identity and consent. Government-issued photo ID must be verified. Privacy consent (PIPEDA) and credit bureau authorization must be signed before any information is processed.

✅

Suitability Proof

Evidence the mortgage suits the borrower's financial situation. Income verification, asset documentation, and liability cross-checks must demonstrate the borrower can service the debt. This protects both the borrower and the brokerage.

🏛

Federal Compliance

AML (FINTRAC) and privacy (PIPEDA) requirements must be met on every file. FINTRAC declarations must be completed. PIPEDA consent must be signed. These are federal legal obligations that apply regardless of province.

🗂

Record Keeping

Proper documentation must be maintained for audit and regulatory review. Files must be organized, complete, and retrievable. Canadian mortgage brokerages are subject to regulatory audits and must be able to produce complete files on demand.

💡 Your broker's compliance team sets the province-specific forms and timelines. Your job is to apply these five principles to every file and flag anything that does not meet them before the file leaves your desk.

Common Errors & Best Practices

The most frequent compliance failures — and how to prevent every one of them.

These are the compliance errors that appear most frequently on mortgage files. Each one is preventable. Knowing them in advance is your first line of defence against rejections, audit findings, and regulatory issues.

7 Common Compliance Errors
!

Mismatch Between Mortgage Approval and Disclosure

The rate, term, or amortization in the disclosure documents does not match what is on the commitment letter. This is a direct disclosure failure.

!

Missing Borrower Initials on Disclosure Pages

Disclosure pages require initials, not just a signature at the end. Missing initials mean the borrower has not confirmed they read each page.

!

Outdated Income or Bank Statements

Pay stubs older than 30 days or bank statements older than 90 days will be rejected. Always check document dates before uploading.

!

Incorrect Legal Names vs. ID

The borrower's legal name on the mortgage documents does not match their government-issued ID. Even a middle initial difference is a compliance error.

!

Missing AML or Consent Documentation

FINTRAC declarations or PIPEDA privacy consent forms are missing from the file. These are federal requirements — there are no exceptions.

!

Incomplete Provincial Disclosure Forms

Province-specific forms are present but not fully completed or signed. Partially completed forms are treated as missing forms by regulators.

!

Missing Audit Trail or File Notes

No record of key decisions, communications, or file updates. Without an audit trail, the file cannot be defended in a regulatory review.

5 Best Practices to Prevent Every Error

✅ What to Do Every Time

  • Reconcile commitment letter vs. compliance package before submitting
  • Use a standardized checklist for every single file — no exceptions
  • Ensure consistent naming conventions across all documents
  • Maintain a clear audit trail with dated notes for every file decision
  • Review files completely before sending to lender or compliance team

❌ Never Do This

  • Never assume a document is current without checking the date
  • Never accept a document that appears edited or altered
  • Never submit without verifying signatures and initials are complete
  • Never skip the final QC because you are under time pressure
  • Never use a nickname or short form of a borrower's legal name

Audit Readiness & Final Submission

A file is audit-ready when all documents are complete, consistent, signed, and traceable.

A file is audit-ready when all documents are complete, consistent, signed, and traceable. Follow this final submission workflow for every file. A fully compliant file protects the borrower, the brokerage, and the broker.

What "Audit-Ready" Means in Practice

✅ Complete

  • Every required document is present
  • All forms are filled in completely
  • No blank fields on any required form

🔁 Consistent

  • Names match across all documents
  • Numbers match between disclosure and approval
  • Dates are current and within acceptable windows

🔍 Traceable

  • Audit trail shows who did what and when
  • File notes document key decisions
  • Submission date and time are logged
Final Submission Workflow — Follow This Every Time
1

Complete the compliance checklist — line by line, no shortcuts

2

Perform final QC review — cross-check all numbers and confirm all names match ID

3

Confirm lender requirements are met — every lender has a specific checklist

4

Upload to lender or compliance system — only when directed by the broker

5

Save the complete package in internal audit folder — immediately after submission

6

Log completion for audit tracking — record date, time, and who submitted

Ontario-Specific Reminders

🏙 Ontario File Compliance Notes

  • Cost of Borrowing Disclosure is mandatory under MBLAA — must be signed before commitment
  • Brokerage disclosure of compensation and services must be in every Ontario file
  • For condo purchases: confirm Status Certificate has been received and is in the file
  • Standard Charge Terms (SCT) reference number must appear on all Ontario mortgage documents
  • All FINTRAC AML declarations must be completed — Ontario regulators audit these specifically
✅ A fully compliant file protects the borrower, the brokerage, and the broker — every time. Your thoroughness is the last line of defence before the file reaches the lender or regulator.
